  • Citric Acid Cycle (big picture)


  • conversion of pyruvate to acetyl-CoA

        --Catalyzed by the pyruvate dehydrogenase complex
        --Requires 5 coenzymes: TPP (thiamine pyrophosphate), lipoyllysine, and FAD are prosthetic groups, NAD+ and CoA-SH  are co-substrate 

        --pyruvate dehydrogenase complex (cryo-EM model)

             --PDC is a large (up to 10 MDa) multienzyme complex composed of E1, E2 and E3
             --Advantages of multienzyme complexes:
                a.short distance between catalytic sites allows channeling of substrates from one catalytic site to another, channeling minimizes side reactions
                b.regulation of activity of one sub-unit affects the entire complex


  • Description of the function of E1, E2 and E3

        a. Enzyme 1 (E1)
                --Step 1: Decarboxylation of pyruvate to an aldehyde
                --Step 2: Oxidation of aldehyde to a carboxylic acid, Electrons reduce lipoamide and form a thioester
        b. Enzyme 2 (E2)
                --Step 3: Formation of acetyl-CoA (product 1)
        c. Enzyme 3 (E3)
                --Step 4: Reoxidation of the lipoamide cofactor
                --Step 5: Regeneration of the oxidized FAD cofactor, forming NADH (product 2)
