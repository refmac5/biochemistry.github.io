The extracellular matrix is composed of an interlocking meshwork of heteropolysaccharides and fibrous proteins such as collagen, elastin, fibronectin, and laminin.



Evidence also shows they can affect the activity and stability of proteins and signalling molecules within the matrix. Individual functions of proteoglycans can be attributed to either the protein core or the attached GAG chain. They can also serve as lubricants.
